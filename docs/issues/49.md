# Issue #49 — Spread-mode back face does not paint past 90° (classic / curl / tilt)

## Symptom

When the reader is in spread mode and the active preset is **classic**, **curl**,
or **tilt**, the flipping leaf rotates around the spine but never reveals the
**new** page content on its back face. The leaf appears empty (or shows a
mirrored front face) once it crosses 90°, and at cleanup the next spread snaps
in. Single-mode flips and the non-rotational presets (slide, fade) are not
affected.

## Root Cause

The single-leaf architecture from #45 builds the leaf with two `<img>` faces:

- a **front** face holding the outgoing page content
- a **back** face pre-rotated `rotateY(180deg)` so it appears mirrored at rest
  and reads correctly once the leaf is past 90°

For this to work, the leaf wrapper needs `transform-style: preserve-3d` so the
back face's own rotation composes with the wrapper's rotation in 3D space.

The wrapper carried `overflow-hidden` (so its rounded corners would clip). Per
the CSS Transforms spec, an element with `overflow` other than `visible` is
**forced to render as if `transform-style: flat`** — even when `preserve-3d`
is declared. With a flat rendering context, the back face's `rotateY(180deg)`
no longer composes with the wrapper's rotation, so the back face never turns
toward the viewer.

Reproduction was deterministic in spread mode with any of `classic`, `curl`,
`tilt`. Slide and fade do not exercise the back face, so they were unaffected
and masked the bug during the #45 review.

## Fix

In `src/components/reader/PageFlip.tsx`'s `OutgoingSpreadLeaf`:

- Removed `overflow-hidden` from the leaf wrapper. The wrapper now only carries
  positioning, `will-change-transform`, and the inline `transformStyle:
  'preserve-3d'` style. The wrapper's children (the two `<img>` faces) are
  `absolute inset-0 h-full w-full object-cover`, so they fit the wrapper
  exactly and have nothing to overflow.
- Moved the rounded-corner clipping from the wrapper down to each `<img>` face.
  Each face now carries `roundClass` directly. Because the images are sized to
  the wrapper's bounds, `border-radius` on the image is enough to mask the
  corners without breaking the 3D rendering context.

With `preserve-3d` preserved, the back face math works:

- Wrapper at `rotateY(0)`: front face faces viewer; back face is at net
  `rotateY(180)` (away), hidden by `backface-visibility: hidden`.
- Wrapper at `rotateY(-90)`: both faces edge-on, invisible.
- Wrapper at `rotateY(-180)`: front face at net `rotateY(-180)`, hidden; back
  face at net `rotateY(0)` (toward viewer), visible.

The phantom layer on the opposite half keeps its `overflow-hidden + roundClass`
because it is a flat layer that never composes 3D transforms.

## Regression Coverage

- `src/components/reader/PageFlip.test.tsx`:
  - "leaf wrapper keeps preserve-3d and never sets overflow-hidden so the back
    face can paint past 90°"
  - "rounded class is applied to the leaf faces (not the wrapper) so 3D
    rendering survives clipping"
- `e2e/reader-spread-flip.spec.ts`:
  - "classic preset back face shows the new page content past halfway in spread
    mode" — asserts `transformStyle === 'preserve-3d'`, `overflow !== 'hidden'`,
    and that the back-face `<img>` carries a `src` distinct from the front
    face's.
